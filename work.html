A major health foundation needed to shift its public narrative from traditional health programming to the emerging climate-health nexus—without alienating existing donors.

The Challenge

Two decades of brand equity were anchored to a programming model the board had voted to evolve. Every draft of the new story either scared loyal donors or undersold the pivot.

Our Approach

A full narrative audit, followed by a strategic framing sprint that positioned climate-health convergence as the continuation of the fund's founding promise—not a departure from it.

The Outcome

A message architecture the CEO, board, and program teams could all deliver—consistently—across fundraising, media, and policy settings.

  • 3× increase in media coverage
  • New institutional partnerships
  • Board alignment achieved

An edtech platform serving 2M students needed a narrative that could raise a Series B while staying true to its community-first mission.

The Challenge

Investors read the company as a charity; communities read investor language as betrayal. The same story had to carry conviction in both rooms.

Our Approach

We built a two-audience message architecture and embedded it across the pitch deck, website, founder keynotes, and internal onboarding—then trained the leadership team to hold the line.

The Outcome

A single, ownable narrative that scaled from village classrooms to institutional term sheets without changing its spine.

  • Lead feature in major publications
  • Series B fully subscribed
  • Investor confidence measurably increased

An intermediary channeling capital to frontline climate projects was losing funder confidence—not because of results, but because no two spokespeople told the same story.

The Challenge

Years of reactive communication had left a patchwork of conflicting decks, reports, and talking points. Funders sensed the incoherence and read it as risk.

Our Approach

A forensic narrative audit—every artifact, every stakeholder interview—surfaced the story the organization had been living but never writing down. We codified it into one foundational narrative.

The Outcome

Leadership finally understood what they were actually trying to say—and funders, partners, and communities heard the same organization for the first time.

  • Unified story across 40+ touchpoints
  • Funder renewal conversations accelerated
  • Leadership voice alignment achieved

A family philanthropy committing ten years to gender-lens investing needed a narrative built for the long arc—one that could hold steady through leadership changes, market cycles, and shifting discourse.

The Challenge

Ten-year commitments outlive news cycles. The philanthropy needed language durable enough to stay recognizable in year eight, yet flexible enough to absorb what the field would learn along the way.

Our Approach

We serve as ongoing narrative counsel—setting the decade's theory of change into a story spine, then advising quarterly as new grants, data, and moments test it.

The Outcome

Three years in, the narrative has survived a leadership transition and two strategy refreshes without losing its throughline.

  • Narrative consistency across 3 years
  • Co-funder coalition grew 2×
  • Field-recognized thought leadership
